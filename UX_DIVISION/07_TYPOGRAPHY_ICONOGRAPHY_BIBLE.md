# ATLAS Typography & Iconography Bible

**Version:** 1.0  
**Classification:** Core Visual Standard

## Purpose

Typography and iconography form the silent visual language of ATLAS.

They must make information easier to scan, understand, remember, and use across every device and workspace.

## Typography Principles

- Readability before style
- Consistent hierarchy
- Stable alignment
- Comfortable spacing
- Scalable presentation
- Strong accessibility

## Type Hierarchy

### Level 1

Application titles, workspace names, and major dashboard headings.

### Level 2

Section headings, laboratory names, project categories, and primary navigation.

### Level 3

Panel headings, card titles, major controls, and chapter headings.

### Level 4

Body copy, documentation, learning material, and research notes.

### Level 5

Captions, metadata, file details, version numbers, and supporting information.

## Alignment Rules

- Left alignment is the default.
- Centered text is used sparingly.
- Right alignment is reserved primarily for numerical values and measurements.

## Icon Principles

Icons must use:

- Simple geometry
- Consistent line weight
- Recognizable silhouettes
- Balanced proportions
- Minimal unnecessary detail

## Interactive States

- Hover: gentle illumination
- Selected: slight expansion and stronger contrast
- Active: brief controlled pulse
- Completed: soft confirmation glow
- Disabled: reduced contrast without disappearing

## Accessibility

Typography must support scalable text, high contrast, screen readers, readable spacing, and clear focus states.

Status must never depend on color alone. Icons, labels, shape, and motion should reinforce meaning.

## Final Principle

The best typography disappears into the reading experience.

The best icon communicates before the user needs an explanation.
