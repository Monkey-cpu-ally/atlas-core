# ATLAS Sound Design Bible

**Version:** 1.0  
**Classification:** Core UX Standard

## Purpose

Sound is one of the primary ways ATLAS communicates state, urgency, completion, presence, and identity.

Audio must improve understanding without competing for attention.

Silence is part of the design system.

## Audio Principles

1. **Meaning** — every sound communicates useful information.
2. **Simplicity** — sounds remain short, clear, and recognizable.
3. **Consistency** — the same event uses the same sound language.
4. **Comfort** — sounds must remain pleasant during long sessions.
5. **Identity** — users should recognize ATLAS by sound alone.

## Sound Categories

- Startup
- Shutdown
- Confirmation
- Notification
- Warning
- Error
- Success
- AI presence
- Environmental ambience
- Robotics safety

## Priority Levels

### Informational

Quiet, brief, and non-intrusive.

### Success

Warm and satisfying without becoming celebratory noise.

### Warning

Clear enough to prompt review, but not panic.

### Critical

Distinct, persistent, and impossible to miss while remaining controlled.

## AI Voice Identity

### Ajani

Deep, measured, confident, and deliberate.

### Minerva

Warm, clear, patient, and educational.

### Hermes

Creative, energetic, curious, and engineering-focused.

### Council

Balanced, unified, and deliberate without excessive echo or robotic effects.

## Accessibility

Every audio event must have a visual equivalent.

Users must be able to:

- Control category volume
- Disable selected sound groups
- Enable captions and transcripts
- Review missed alerts
- Use the system without sound

## Final Principle

ATLAS audio should quietly build confidence.

The best sound is useful, recognizable, and comfortable enough that users appreciate it without being distracted by it.
