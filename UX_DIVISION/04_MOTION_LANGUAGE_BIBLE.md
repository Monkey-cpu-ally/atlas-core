# ATLAS Motion Language Bible

**Version:** 1.0  
**Classification:** UX Division Standard

## Purpose

Motion is one of the primary languages of ATLAS.

Before a user reads text or hears speech, the interface communicates through movement.

Every animation must answer: what is the system trying to tell the user?

## Motion Philosophy

ATLAS should feel like a living system, not a mechanical screen.

The interface should breathe, settle, respond, and guide attention without becoming distracting.

## Five Laws of Motion

### 1. Purpose

Every animation must communicate state, progress, focus, selection, warning, discovery, or completion.

### 2. Smoothness

Movement begins softly, accelerates naturally, and stops without abruptness.

### 3. Continuity

Objects should move from one location to another. The user should understand where things came from and where they went.

### 4. Balance

Only one major motion should dominate the screen at a time.

### 5. Silence

Stillness is part of motion. When nothing important is happening, the interface should remain calm.

## Ring Movement

HUD rings move slowly and independently.

They should resemble astronomical systems rather than gears.

No fast spinning.

No meaningless constant motion.

## AI Activation Motion

When an AI activates:

- Portrait brightens
- Core emits synchronized pulse
- Voice waveform appears
- Selected AI color intensifies
- Other AIs remain visible but secondary

## Thinking Animation

Never use a generic loading spinner for reasoning.

Use:

- Knowledge particles
- Connection lines
- Core pulses
- Illuminated pathways
- Subtle network motion

## Warning Motion

Warnings use amber pulses, controlled vibration, edge lighting, and focused direction.

Avoid aggressive flashing.

## Critical Motion

Critical alerts must be persistent, clear, and unmistakable without causing panic.

## Performance Rule

Animations must never reduce usability.

If performance drops, reduce particles and lighting before reducing responsiveness.

## Final Principle

Motion should guide, reassure, educate, and inspire confidence.

The best motion is felt before it is noticed.
