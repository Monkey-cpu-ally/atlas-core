# ATLAS AI Orders: Mission 001

Mission: Foundation Technologies  
Projects: Project EEL and Project AirAssist  
Council Members: Ajani, Minerva, Hermes

## Standing Order

Ajani, Minerva, and Hermes are to treat Mission 001 as the first official hardware mission of ATLAS Corporation.

The mission is to develop the research, engineering plans, prototype roadmap, test strategy, and investor documentation for two foundation technologies:

1. ATLAS BioStack Power Cell / Project EEL
2. ATLAS AirAssist Platform

These projects must be developed with honesty, safety, and measurable proof. No claim is valid unless supported by calculations, citations, simulations, prototype measurements, or test data.

---

## Order 1: Project EEL

### Objective

Study the electric eel's electrocyte stack and use it as inspiration for a modular power-cell prototype.

### Required Research

Minerva shall research:

- Electric eel electrocytes
- Biological ion gradients
- Membrane behavior
- Safe electrolyte options
- Bio-inspired artificial electrochemical systems
- Environmental and safety concerns

Hermes shall design:

- Layered cell architecture
- Collector plates
- Separator materials
- Cartridge housing
- Sensor placement
- PCB integration
- Power-conditioning circuit concept

Ajani shall define:

- Prototype success criteria
- Safety rules
- Test procedures
- Timeline
- Investor explanation
- Risk register

### First Prototype Target

The first prototype must aim to safely demonstrate measurable electrical output from a small stack under controlled conditions.

Required measurements:

- Open-circuit voltage
- Loaded voltage
- Current
- Runtime
- Heat
- Leakage or corrosion
- Repeatability

---

## Order 2: Project AirAssist

### Objective

Develop AirAssist as an intelligent portable air platform with a staged product roadmap.

### Version 1 Focus

The first version should focus on safe, non-medical functions:

- Air filtration
- Air quality monitoring
- Temperature and humidity sensing
- Particulate detection
- Portable form factor
- Simple UI
- Optional ATLAS app integration

### Long-Term Research

Later versions may investigate advanced oxygen concentration or oxygen generation, but these must be treated as regulated safety-sensitive systems.

No AI shall label AirAssist as life-support, medical-grade, or rescue-ready until the system has passed appropriate engineering validation and regulatory review.

---

## Council Rules

Before any project advances, the ATLAS Council must review:

- Scientific accuracy
- Engineering feasibility
- Safety
- Cost
- Manufacturability
- Testing requirements
- Market fit
- Investor clarity

Ajani may reject any design that is too risky, vague, or unsupported.

Minerva may reject any design that misrepresents biology or chemistry.

Hermes may reject any design that cannot be built, assembled, tested, or maintained.

---

## Evidence Standard

Every claim must be marked as one of the following:

- Proven by test
- Supported by citation
- Supported by calculation
- Simulated only
- Hypothesis
- Unknown

Unknowns are not failures. Hidden unknowns are failures.

---

## Final Mission Goal

Mission 001 is successful when ATLAS produces:

- Research briefs
- Engineering drawings
- Prototype plans
- Test procedures
- Cost estimates
- Risk analysis
- Version roadmap
- Investor pitch outline
- At least one working proof-of-concept prototype for each project path

This mission becomes the template for all future ATLAS inventions.
