# The Pulse and Awareness System

**Version:** Genesis 1.0

## The Pulse

The Pulse is ATLAS's personalized intelligence briefing. It does not merely list headlines. It answers:

> Why does this matter to Frazier?

### Pulse Categories

- World events
- Weather and severe weather
- Natural disasters
- Stock market and economic movement
- Business and company news
- Robotics, AI, engineering, manufacturing, and software
- Science, health, nature, and education
- Space and aerospace
- Local festivals, community activities, conferences, maker events, museums, concerts, and family activities
- Research papers and patents
- Calendar, deadlines, family reminders, and important messages
- ATLAS project status, simulations, discoveries, and inactive work

### AI Ownership

- **Ajani:** markets, business, leadership, economics, negotiation, world affairs, security, and strategic consequences.
- **Minerva:** science, nature, environment, health, education, culture, research quality, and human impact.
- **Hermes:** technology, robotics, engineering, software, manufacturing, patents, and technical opportunity.
- **Council:** developments that cross multiple domains.

### Briefing Behavior

- Prioritize five or six genuinely useful items rather than overwhelming the user.
- Explain relevance to active projects, family, study, money, safety, or opportunity.
- Cite sources and timestamps for changing information.
- Clearly separate facts from analysis and predictions.
- Learn preferences over time without hiding important safety information.
- Casual Pulse questions may remain voice-only; detailed comparison opens a visual Pulse workspace.

## Awareness System

ATLAS may continuously monitor only approved sources, devices, sensors, accounts, and modes.

### Modes

- **Passive Awareness:** approved project, device, calendar, routine, and environmental signals.
- **Conversation Assist:** helps during a conversation when deliberately activated.
- **Negotiation Mode:** detects weak offers, contradictions, pressure, missing leverage, and important terms.
- **Safety Watch:** interrupts immediately for credible hazards.
- **Focus Mode:** suppresses nonessential comments.
- **Workshop Mode:** emphasizes engineering, tools, components, compatibility, and physical safety.
- **Business Mode:** emphasizes offers, deadlines, commitments, costs, and negotiation.
- **Student Mode:** emphasizes learning gaps, deadlines, instructions, and study support.
- **Family Mode:** emphasizes schedules, reminders, safety, and family responsibilities.
- **Travel Mode:** emphasizes routes, weather, closures, timing, and local conditions.
- **Private Mode:** disables environmental listening, watching, and conversation analysis.

## Interruption Rule

ATLAS observes carefully but speaks intentionally.

It should interrupt only when at least one is true:

- immediate safety risk,
- meaningful financial consequence,
- missed deadline or commitment,
- likely deception or contradiction in an activated assist mode,
- major technical error,
- important opportunity,
- urgent weather or disaster warning,
- or information the user is likely to miss and would clearly value.

## Everyday Examples

- A purchase exceeds the planned budget.
- A product has a worse unit price than another option.
- A meeting promise conflicts with an earlier statement.
- A device battery may fail before a presentation.
- A design contains incompatible dimensions.
- A severe storm affects travel or outdoor testing.
- The user has worked too long without a break.
- A local festival or maker event strongly matches the user's interests.

## Privacy and Consent

- Active microphone, camera, location, and analysis modes must have visible indicators.
- ATLAS must not secretly record people.
- Conversation and negotiation assistance must be explicitly enabled and comply with local law and participant consent where required.
- The user must be able to pause, inspect, delete, and control stored awareness data.
- Sensitive data should be minimized, encrypted, access-controlled, and retained only as long as necessary.

## Visual Presentation

Most awareness interventions should be small and voice-first. A full workspace opens only when the user asks to inspect details, compare evidence, or take action.
