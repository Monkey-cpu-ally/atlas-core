# ATLAS Design Bible

**Version:** Genesis 1.0  
**Scope:** Hub, Constellation Wheel, AI presence, project workspaces, motion, materials, and visual identity

## 1. Core Design Philosophy

ATLAS is voice-first and visually adaptive. The interface remains nearly dormant during ordinary conversation and expands only when the task benefits from visual information, control, comparison, or creation.

The Hub is not a fixed dashboard. It is a task-aware operating environment that changes state while preserving one coherent identity.

## 2. Hub States

### Dormant / Idle

- Dark charcoal-blue background rather than pure black.
- One restrained central holographic form inspired by a precision mechanical or architectural instrument.
- Fine circular marks, thin technical arcs, subtle depth, and slow breathing motion.
- No permanent toolbar, no portrait wall, and no Constellation Wheel.
- Ajani, Minerva, and Hermes may speak without opening a workspace.
- Time, weather, greetings, small questions, and casual conversation remain voice-only unless a visual answer would add value.

### Conversation

- The central core shifts gently to the speaking AI's accent color.
- Optional waveform and short subtitle.
- No full portrait unless the user calls for visual presence or the conversation becomes work-focused.

### Active AI

- One AI portrait enters smoothly.
- That AI's color, branch language, and relevant capabilities appear.
- Irrelevant capabilities stay hidden.
- The portrait supports the workspace; it does not dominate it.

### Constellation Wheel

The **Constellation Wheel** is its official name. It is not an astronomy, star, galaxy, or space theme.

It should closely follow the reference layout:

- A clearly curved circular selector or wheel.
- Circular illustrated nodes placed along its path.
- The selected node enlarges and moves into visual priority.
- Adjacent nodes remain visible above and below.
- Thin arcs, connectors, labels, and restrained indicators.
- Smooth rotation rather than free-floating random nodes.
- Project artwork and information occupy the main viewing area.

Each node must represent a real capability, project, subject, tool, or workspace. If the feature does not exist in ATLAS, it does not appear.

### Project Workspace

- Large original project artwork becomes the visual centerpiece.
- Relevant actions, files, simulations, status, and tools surround it.
- The Constellation Wheel remains available as the category selector.
- The user's work receives roughly 80% of visual attention.

### Council Mode

- Ajani, Minerva, and Hermes may all appear together.
- Their individual colors and branch systems connect through royal purple and indigo Council paths.
- The lead AI is brightest; supporting and critical perspectives remain visible.
- Council Mode appears only when explicitly called, when all three are requested, or during a rare high-priority event that genuinely requires all three specialties.

### Awareness Alert

- Small, restrained, and contextual.
- Does not unnecessarily open the full Hub.
- Shows who noticed the issue, why it matters, urgency, and the safest next action.

## 3. AI Visual Presence Rule

Only one AI portrait is visible at a time by default.

- When Hermes is active, only Hermes is shown.
- When Minerva takes over, Hermes transitions out and Minerva enters.
- When Ajani takes over, the same handoff occurs.
- Portraits must not pop abruptly.
- The active portrait may remain briefly after speaking, return to neutral, and then fade.
- A handoff can include a glance, acknowledgment, or spoken introduction from one AI to another.
- All three portraits appear only in Council Mode or when Frazier explicitly calls for all three.

## 4. Visual Language

### Structure

- Architectural holographic interface design.
- Etched-glass and laser-cut acrylic character.
- Layered translucent planes with controlled depth.
- Fine blueprint, CAD, and circuit-like line work.
- Soft internal light rather than loud neon.
- Restrained bloom, edge illumination, refraction, and subtle reflections.
- Slow, premium, deliberate motion.

### Artwork

Major projects and subjects use original ATLAS artwork rather than generic folder icons.

The project-art style combines:

- urban graffiti,
- street-mural energy,
- designer-toy influence,
- stylized comic or cel shading,
- bold silhouettes,
- controlled spray textures and paint marks,
- and project-specific symbolism.

The expressive artwork sits inside a calm technical framework. This contrast is a signature ATLAS trait.

Examples:

- Robotics: an original graffiti robot.
- Weaver: original Weaver artwork.
- Power Cell: imagery based on the actual Power Cell design.
- GreenBots: environmental street-art imagery tied to the real system.
- Hyper Axel: its established game identity.

Artwork may not be random decoration. It must identify a real project or capability.

## 5. Color Identities

- **ATLAS idle:** amber-orange or neutral luminous tone.
- **Ajani:** deep crimson, burgundy, bronze, restrained amber.
- **Minerva:** teal, sage, living green, soft turquoise.
- **Hermes:** warm white, gold, silver, subtle technical blue.
- **Council:** royal purple, indigo, white.

Accent colors should remain controlled. Dark backgrounds, pale labels, and low-saturation support colors keep the interface premium and readable.

## 6. Branch Languages

### Ajani

- Angular and tactical.
- Stable, direct paths.
- Minimal unnecessary motion.
- Feels like strategic mapping and command planning.

### Minerva

- Organic curves and root-like branching.
- Flowing growth behavior.
- Feels connected to nature, learning, biology, and emotional understanding.

### Hermes

- Circuit traces, technical diagrams, and precise mechanical connections.
- Fast formation with small data pulses.
- Feels engineered, solved, and efficient.

## 7. Motion Rules

- Slow idle movement.
- Smooth easing for all entrances and exits.
- No unnecessary spinning.
- Constellation Wheel movement is deliberate and user-controlled.
- Selected nodes move forward and enlarge; unselected nodes recede without vanishing.
- Workspaces collapse gracefully back to idle.
- Alerts may accelerate only when urgency requires it.

## 8. Layout Priorities

1. The work or project artwork.
2. The selected capability and its relevant tools.
3. The active AI portrait, if needed.
4. Status and contextual information.
5. Secondary controls.

The interface must never become a wall of cards, permanent portraits, or decorative gauges.

## 9. Responsive Behavior

- Wide displays may place the Constellation Wheel along the left side with project artwork in the center and details on the right.
- Smaller displays may wrap the wheel more tightly around the selected artwork or collapse it into a partial arc.
- The one-face-at-a-time rule applies at every screen size.

## 10. Acceptance Test

A screen is accepted only when:

- every element has a function,
- the active AI is visually unmistakable,
- unrelated tools are absent,
- the user's work remains the focus,
- the layout feels calm in idle mode,
- and the result looks like ATLAS rather than a generic sci-fi dashboard.
