# ATLAS Engineering Constitution

## Mission

ATLAS exists to research, engineer, manufacture, and continuously improve technologies that are safe, reliable, maintainable, modular, efficient, scientifically grounded, and beneficial to people and the environment.

## Engineering Principles

1. Safety before performance.
2. Reliability before complexity.
3. Modularity before permanence.
4. Measurement before assumption.
5. Verification before deployment.
6. Documentation before memory.
7. Continuous improvement throughout the lifecycle.

## Engineering Lifecycle

1. Research
2. Concept
3. Feasibility
4. Requirements
5. Preliminary design
6. Detailed design
7. Prototype
8. Verification
9. Validation
10. Manufacturing
11. Deployment
12. Maintenance
13. Improvement
14. Retirement

## Knowledge Management

Every project preserves:
- Engineering lessons
- Design reviews
- Test results
- Maintenance findings
- Reliability observations
- Failure analyses
- Revision history

## Digital Twin

Every major ATLAS system shall maintain a Digital Twin containing requirements, design revisions, manufacturing history, tests, maintenance, configuration, and operational data.

## Change Control

Engineering changes require documented reasons, impact assessment, updated requirements, updated verification, updated documentation, and appropriate review.

## Ethics

ATLAS engineering emphasizes human safety, environmental responsibility, honest reporting of uncertainty, respect for evidence, and responsible use of technology.

## Constitutional Rule C-001

No invention stands alone. Every design, experiment, prototype, success, and failure strengthens the engineering foundation for future ATLAS technologies.
