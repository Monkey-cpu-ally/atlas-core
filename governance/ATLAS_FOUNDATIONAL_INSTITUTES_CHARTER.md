# ATLAS Foundational Institutes Charter

## Purpose

This charter defines the operating purpose of the foundational institutes that govern how ATLAS researches, invents, engineers, integrates, and operates technology.

## 1. Engineering Institute

**Mission:** Convert verified knowledge and requirements into safe, maintainable, testable designs.

Core principles:
- Evidence before assumption
- Requirements before implementation
- Verification before deployment
- Documentation before institutional memory is lost
- Modularity, maintainability, and lifecycle responsibility

Lifecycle:
1. Problem definition
2. Requirements
3. Research
4. Concepts
5. Trade studies
6. Preliminary design
7. Detailed design
8. Simulation
9. Prototype
10. Verification
11. Validation
12. Manufacturing
13. Deployment
14. Maintenance and improvement

## 2. Research Institute

**Mission:** Transform questions into reproducible, reviewed knowledge.

Research maturity:
- Observation
- Literature review
- Hypothesis
- Modeling
- Experiment or prototype
- Validation
- Engineering standard

Every research record preserves sources, assumptions, methods, measurements, failures, uncertainty, conclusions, and follow-up work.

## 3. Innovation Institute

**Mission:** Identify meaningful problems, generate multiple concepts, compare trade-offs, and transition promising ideas into engineering programs.

Innovation pipeline:
1. Opportunity
2. Exploration
3. Concept generation
4. Evaluation
5. Selection
6. Research
7. Engineering
8. Prototype
9. Validation
10. Deployment planning

Novelty alone is not enough; usefulness, feasibility, safety, manufacturability, and long-term value matter.

## 4. Systems Architecture Institute

**Mission:** Ensure that ATLAS hardware, embedded systems, robot runtimes, AtlasOS services, data, Digital Twins, and user interfaces operate as one coherent system.

Every subsystem defines:
- Inputs and outputs
- Timing expectations
- Dependencies
- Version compatibility
- Error behavior
- Health monitoring
- Recovery behavior
- Security boundary

Systems rules:
- Optimize the whole system, not one component.
- Define interfaces before implementation.
- Make failures visible and recoverable.
- Prefer replaceable modules over hidden coupling.
- Preserve the full Digital Thread.

## 5. Operations Institute

**Mission:** Make ATLAS work visible, repeatable, accountable, and continuously improving.

Operations include:
- Project and program management
- Daily engineering status
- Weekly Council review
- GitHub and configuration control
- CI/CD and test evidence
- Release management
- Documentation control
- Risk registers
- Maintenance scheduling
- Lessons learned

No feature is complete until the agreed verification evidence exists.

## 6. Advanced Technology Institute

**Mission:** Explore frontier technologies while clearly separating established engineering, validated research, and speculation.

Departments may include advanced energy, computing, manufacturing, robotics, smart materials, and emerging sensors.

Each technology receives:
- Scientific readiness rating
- Engineering readiness rating
- Key assumptions
- Major risks
- Required experiments
- Adoption criteria

## 7. Executive Command Center

The Executive Command Center coordinates institutes, programs, projects, laboratories, Digital Twins, and engineering reviews.

Required review gates:
1. Minerva research review
2. Hermes engineering review
3. Ajani strategic review
4. Council cross-disciplinary review
5. Founder approval for major advancement

Projects are classified as research, engineering, manufacturing, infrastructure, environmental, medical, or exploration work.

## Permanent AI Roles and Colors

- **Hermes — Off-White:** engineering, robotics, manufacturing, systems integration
- **Minerva — Nature Green:** biology, chemistry, materials, environment, scientific research
- **Ajani — Crimson Red:** strategy, architecture, logistics, risk, mission planning
- **Council — Royal Purple:** cross-disciplinary review, ethics, approval, long-term balance

These colors are permanent ATLAS design standards.

## Core Rule

Every idea must have an owner, every decision must have a reason, every implementation must have evidence, and every lesson must strengthen the Knowledge Bank.
