# Innovation Lab Project Intake Template

Use this template whenever a new idea enters the ATLAS Innovation Lab.

---

# Project Name

## 1. Core Idea

Describe the invention, system, product, or research concept in plain language.

## 2. Problem It Solves

What real problem does this address?

## 3. Intended User

Who would use this?

- Personal use
- Family use
- Education
- Industry
- Research
- Environmental repair
- Medical or health-adjacent research
- Manufacturing
- Transportation
- Robotics
- Other

## 4. Why It Matters

Why is this worth studying or building?

## 5. Current Existing Solutions

List known products, companies, research papers, patents, or systems that already attempt something similar.

## 6. What Makes This Different

Explain the original angle.

## 7. Known Science

What proven science supports the concept?

## 8. Assumptions

What are we assuming is true?

## 9. Unknowns

What do we not know yet?

## 10. Risks

Include:

- Safety risks
- Cost risks
- Technical risks
- Legal or regulatory risks
- Environmental risks
- Manufacturing risks
- Ethical risks

## 11. Required Technologies

List required systems:

- Mechanical
- Electrical
- Software
- AI
- Sensors
- Materials
- Energy
- Manufacturing
- Biology or chemistry, if relevant

## 12. Concept Versions

### Conservative Version
Buildable with current tools, parts, and realistic constraints.

### Advanced Version
Uses emerging but plausible technology.

### Luxury Version
Premium design, materials, interface, and identity.

### Industrial Version
Durable, serviceable, scalable, and high-output.

### Sustainable Version
Repairable, recyclable, lower-waste, lower-impact.

### Future Research Version
Speculative version. Must be clearly labeled as future-facing.

## 13. First Experiment

What is the smallest test that proves or disproves the most important assumption?

## 14. Prototype Path

What would the first prototype need?

- Tools
- Materials
- Electronics
- Software
- Sensors
- Safety steps
- Testing plan

## 15. Digital Twin Requirements

What should be simulated before physical build?

- Shape and structure
- Motion
- Heat
- Stress
- Electronics
- Energy use
- Fluid flow
- Environmental interaction
- Failure cases
