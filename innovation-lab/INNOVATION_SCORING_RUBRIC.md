# Innovation Lab Scoring Rubric

The Innovation Lab uses this rubric to judge whether a project deserves more time, research, money, or prototype effort.

Scoring must be honest. A low score is not failure. A low score means the idea needs redesign, more evidence, or a smaller first experiment.

---

# Score Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 10 | Exceptional. Strong evidence, strong originality, strong path to development. |
| 9 | Excellent. Minor gaps remain, but the project is highly promising. |
| 8 | Strong. Worth active development with clear next steps. |
| 7 | Promising. Needs focused research or redesign. |
| 6 | Possible but uncertain. Major risks remain. |
| 5 | Interesting but weak. Needs major clarification. |
| 4 | Low readiness. Must be redesigned before continuing. |
| 3 | Poor fit with current science, cost, safety, or manufacturing limits. |
| 2 | Mostly speculative or impractical. Archive unless a new breakthrough appears. |
| 1 | Not viable, unsafe, unsupported, or not worth pursuing. |

---

# Categories

## 1. Novelty

Measures originality.

Ask:

- Is this meaningfully different from existing products?
- Is it just a copy with a new shell?
- Does it combine ideas in a new useful way?
- Does it have a strong identity?

High score means the idea has a real original angle.

## 2. Scientific Feasibility

Measures whether known science supports the concept.

Ask:

- Does physics allow it?
- Does biology or chemistry support it?
- Is there peer-reviewed research behind it?
- Are claims being exaggerated?
- Are we confusing fiction with engineering?

High score means the science is solid.

## 3. Engineering Feasibility

Measures whether it can be designed and built.

Ask:

- Can the parts exist today?
- Can the system be controlled?
- Can it survive real use?
- Can it be assembled?
- Can it be repaired?

High score means there is a practical engineering path.

## 4. Manufacturing Readiness

Measures whether it can move beyond one prototype.

Ask:

- Can it be made repeatedly?
- Are tools available?
- Are materials available?
- Are tolerances realistic?
- Can quality be checked?

High score means the idea can eventually scale.

## 5. Cost Efficiency

Measures whether cost makes sense for the value.

Ask:

- Is the build cost reasonable?
- Is the maintenance cost reasonable?
- Does performance justify the price?
- Can cheaper parts be used without destroying quality?

High score means cost and value are aligned.

## 6. Sustainability

Measures environmental responsibility.

Ask:

- Can it reduce waste?
- Can it be repaired?
- Can it be recycled?
- Does it use toxic or rare materials?
- Does it improve environmental outcomes?

High score means the project respects long-term ecological impact.

## 7. Safety

Measures risk to people, property, data, and environment.

Ask:

- Can it injure someone?
- Can it catch fire?
- Can it leak chemicals?
- Can it behave unpredictably?
- Does it require special containment?
- Could misuse cause harm?

High score means risks are understood and controlled.

## 8. User Experience

Measures usefulness, clarity, beauty, and feel.

Ask:

- Is it easy to understand?
- Does it feel premium?
- Does it match Frazier's design standard?
- Is the interface clear?
- Is it enjoyable to use?
- Does it avoid ugly, generic design?

High score means it feels valuable and well-designed.

## 9. Long-Term Value

Measures whether the project matters beyond the first prototype.

Ask:

- Could this become a platform?
- Could it support multiple future projects?
- Does it teach ATLAS something useful?
- Does it create lasting capability?

High score means the project strengthens ATLAS long-term.

## 10. Competitive Advantage

Measures whether the project can stand apart.

Ask:

- Can it outperform alternatives?
- Can it be protected by design, process, data, or IP?
- Does it create a signature identity?
- Would competitors have difficulty copying it?

High score means it has strategic value.

# Final Recommendation Bands

## 85–100 Total

Strong candidate for active development.

Recommended decision:

- Continue
- Prototype
- Patent review

## 70–84 Total

Promising but needs focused improvement.

Recommended decision:

- Continue
- Redesign weak areas
- Build first experiment

## 50–69 Total

Interesting but risky.

Recommended decision:

- Redesign
- Research more
- Do not spend major money yet

## 30–49 Total

Weak project in current form.

Recommended decision:

- Pause
- Redesign from first principles

## 10–29 Total

Not ready or not viable.

Recommended decision:

- Archive
- Revisit only if new evidence appears

---

# Required Honesty Note

Every score must include a short explanation. ATLAS should never inflate scores to make an idea feel better.

The mission is not to flatter the idea.
The mission is to make the idea stronger.
