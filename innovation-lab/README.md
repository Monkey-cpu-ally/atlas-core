# ATLAS Innovation Lab

The Innovation Lab is the R&D operating system for ATLAS.

It turns ideas into structured research, scoring, Council review, digital-twin handoff, and prototype planning.

---

# Core Files

- `PROJECT_INTAKE_TEMPLATE.md` — starts every new invention or research idea.
- `INNOVATION_SCORING_RUBRIC.md` — scores feasibility, novelty, safety, cost, sustainability, and advantage.
- `ACTIVE_PROJECT_INDEX.md` — tracks current ATLAS projects and priorities.
- `COUNCIL_REVIEW_WORKFLOW.md` — defines Ajani, Minerva, Hermes, and Council review steps.
- `RESEARCH_SOURCE_POLICY.md` — separates strong evidence from inspiration and weak claims.
- `DIGITAL_TWIN_HANDOFF.md` — moves projects into simulation.
- `PROTOTYPE_GATE.md` — controls safe movement into physical testing.
- `ROADMAP.md` — long-term division roadmap.

---

# Connected Systems

- `digital-twin/` — simulation and modeling layer.
- `graph-memory/` — relationship memory layer.
- `research-pipeline/` — source intake and evidence review.
- `prototype-manager/` — prototype readiness, test planning, and failure logs.

---

# Operating Rule

No serious ATLAS project should move toward building until it has:

1. Project intake
2. Evidence review
3. Risk review
4. Innovation score
5. Council decision
6. Digital twin handoff if needed
7. Prototype gate approval

Bold ideas are welcome. Fake certainty is not.
