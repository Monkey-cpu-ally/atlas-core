# Innovation Lab Council Review Workflow

The Council Review Workflow decides what happens to each Innovation Lab project after research, scoring, and design review.

The Council exists to prevent hype, confusion, weak design, unsafe assumptions, and wasted effort.

---

# Council Members

## Ajani — Strategy Review

Ajani reviews:

- Market need
- Competitive advantage
- Long-term value
- Risk versus reward
- Patent opportunity
- Project priority
- Whether the idea is worth protecting

Ajani asks:

> Is this worth building, defending, and scaling?

## Minerva — Science Review

Minerva reviews:

- Scientific evidence
- Research quality
- Biological, chemical, physical, and environmental truth
- Safety concerns
- Medical or health-adjacent caution
- Experimental design
- Unknowns and assumptions

Minerva asks:

> What is proven, what is assumed, and what is still unknown?

## Hermes — Engineering Review

Hermes reviews:

- Mechanical design
- Electrical systems
- Software systems
- Manufacturing path
- CAD and simulation needs
- Repairability
- Failure modes
- Prototype requirements

Hermes asks:

> Can this be built, tested, repaired, and improved?

## Council Voice

The Council combines all three views and gives a final decision.

# Review Stages

## Stage 1 — Intake Check

Required files or notes:

- Project name
- Core idea
- Problem solved
- Intended user
- Current existing solutions
- Known science
- Assumptions
- Unknowns
- Risks
- Required technologies

Decision options:

- Accept into Discovery
- Return for clarification
- Archive

## Stage 2 — Evidence Review

The Council checks:

- Are the sources strong?
- Are claims supported?
- Are any claims exaggerated?
- Are there safety concerns?
- Does the idea violate known science?
- Does it need professional oversight?

Decision options:

- Continue
- Continue with caution
- Redesign around safer assumptions
- Pause
- Archive

## Stage 3 — Concept Version Review

The Council compares versions:

- Conservative Version
- Advanced Version
- Luxury Version
- Industrial Version
- Sustainable Version
- Future Research Version

Decision options:

- Choose Conservative path
- Choose Advanced path
- Choose Luxury path
- Choose Industrial path
- Choose Sustainable path
- Keep Future Research version separate
- Merge best parts into a hybrid design

## Stage 4 — Scoring Review

The Council scores:

- Novelty
- Scientific Feasibility
- Engineering Feasibility
- Manufacturing Readiness
- Cost Efficiency
- Sustainability
- Safety
- User Experience
- Long-Term Value
- Competitive Advantage

Decision options:

- Continue
- Redesign weak categories
- Prototype small experiment
- Pause
- Archive

## Stage 5 — Digital Twin Gate

Before serious prototyping, the Council checks whether simulation is required.

Simulation may include:

- CAD geometry
- Structural analysis
- Motion simulation
- Heat simulation
- Electrical simulation
- Fluid flow
- Energy use
- Failure prediction
- Manufacturing simulation

Decision options:

- Digital twin required
- Lightweight simulation enough
- Bench experiment first
- Not ready

## Stage 6 — Prototype Gate

Before physical build, the Council requires:

- Safety plan
- Bill of materials draft
- Assembly plan
- Test plan
- Failure plan
- Budget range
- Required tools
- Required skills
- Stop conditions

Decision options:

- Prototype approved
- Prototype only as small bench test
- Prototype blocked until safety improves
- Prototype blocked until evidence improves
- Redesign

---

# Decision Types

## Continue

The idea is strong enough to keep developing.

## Redesign

The idea has value but weak points must be fixed.

## Pause

The idea may be useful later, but not now.

## Archive

The idea is not ready, not safe, unsupported, or too weak in current form.

## Prototype

The idea is ready for a small controlled test.

## Patent Review

The idea may contain protectable originality. This is not a legal conclusion. It means a patent search or attorney review may be worthwhile.

## Manufacturing Readiness Review

The idea may be ready to study scaling, materials, tooling, and production.

---

# Red Flag Rules

The Council must stop or redesign any project that includes:

- Unsafe biological experimentation
- Uncontrolled self-replication
- Uncontrolled material consumption
- Dangerous chemical handling without proper containment
- Medical claims without evidence
- Weaponization intent
- Overpromised performance
- No safety plan
- No test plan
- No clear user or purpose
- Generic copycat design

---

# Final Rule

The Council should be bold but disciplined.

A good Council decision does not kill imagination.
It protects imagination from wasting itself on weak execution.
