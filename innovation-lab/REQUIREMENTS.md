# ATLAS Innovation Lab — Requirements

## Purpose
This file defines what the Innovation Lab must do with precision. It prevents the division from becoming a loose pile of ideas.

---

# Functional Requirements

## IL-FR-001 — Project Intake
The Innovation Lab must provide a repeatable intake process for every new invention, research idea, product concept, or engineering system.

A valid intake must include:
- Core idea
- Problem solved
- Intended user
- Known science
- Assumptions
- Unknowns
- Risks
- Required technologies
- First experiment
- Council decision

## IL-FR-002 — Evidence Classification
The Innovation Lab must classify evidence before using it to support decisions.

Evidence must be labeled as:
- Proven
- Strong evidence
- Promising evidence
- Early research
- Plausible assumption
- Speculative
- Unsupported
- Unsafe or rejected

## IL-FR-003 — Innovation Scoring
Every serious project must receive a score across novelty, feasibility, manufacturing, cost, sustainability, safety, user experience, long-term value, and competitive advantage.

## IL-FR-004 — Council Review
Every serious project must be reviewed by Ajani, Minerva, Hermes, and the Council before moving toward physical prototyping.

## IL-FR-005 — Digital Twin Handoff
Projects involving physical systems, robotics, energy, heat, loads, sensors, or manufacturing must be evaluated for Digital Twin handoff.

## IL-FR-006 — Prototype Gate
No physical prototype should be approved without safety risks, stop conditions, test goals, and prototype level.

## IL-FR-007 — Failure Learning
Every failed prototype or rejected design must produce a failure note or lesson learned.

---

# Non-Functional Requirements

## IL-NFR-001 — Clarity
Documents must be understandable to Frazier, useful to AI agents, and structured enough for future software.

## IL-NFR-002 — Precision
No vague claim should be treated as a requirement. If the system does not know something, it must say so.

## IL-NFR-003 — Traceability
Important decisions should connect back to evidence, assumptions, risks, or Council review.

## IL-NFR-004 — Maintainability
Files must have a clear purpose. Duplicate files should be avoided unless they serve a distinct role.

## IL-NFR-005 — Safety
Safety-critical projects require extra review before prototype planning.

---

# Completion Standard

A project is considered Innovation Lab ready when it has:

1. Intake document
2. Evidence notes
3. Risk list
4. Innovation score
5. Council review
6. Digital Twin handoff decision
7. Prototype Gate decision, if physical testing is planned

---

# Quality Rule

ATLAS should be ambitious, but every ambition must have a path, a test, or a clearly marked unknown.
