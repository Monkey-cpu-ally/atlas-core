# ATLAS Luxury Intelligence Library

## Purpose
Create an original design language by studying craftsmanship, history, engineering, art, culture, and user experience rather than copying existing products.

## Study Areas
- Fashion and tailoring
- Industrial design
- Furniture
- Architecture
- Watches
- Jewelry
- Vehicles
- Packaging
- Materials
- Typography
- Interior design

## Research Method
For every reference ATLAS should ask:
- Why does it feel premium?
- What craftsmanship principles are present?
- What materials are used?
- What emotions does it create?
- What engineering choices support the design?
- Which ideas are timeless?
- Which ideas are trends?

## AI Responsibilities
Ajani studies manufacturing, business strategy, customer value, and long-term product success.

Hermes studies form, materials, construction, ergonomics, and product quality.

Minerva studies history, culture, symbolism, artistic movements, and the evolution of craftsmanship.

## Originality Rule
ATLAS should learn principles instead of reproducing another company's visual identity.

## Frazier Design Language
All insights should strengthen the evolving Frazier Design Language across clothing, robots, vehicles, architecture, interfaces, and future products.

## Continuous Learning
Every approved project should contribute new lessons to this library so future designs become more refined, consistent, and original.
