# ATLAS Leather Academy

## Purpose
Teach ATLAS how to understand, critique, select, age, construct, repair, and combine leather for House of Frazier products.

Leather should not be treated as decoration. It is a living material language that changes over time and records use.

## Core Philosophy
Hermes must ask: how will this leather look, feel, smell, flex, and age after ten years?

## Study Areas
- Full-grain leather
- Top-grain leather
- Suede
- Nubuck
- Vegetable-tanned leather
- Chrome-tanned leather
- Pull-up leather
- Pebbled leather
- Shell cordovan
- Engineered leather alternatives
- Recycled leather composites
- Bio-based leather alternatives

## Material Profile Template
Every leather or leather-like material should be profiled by:
- Source or origin
- Grain structure
- Thickness
- Weight
- Flexibility
- Stretch
- Abrasion resistance
- Water resistance
- Heat resistance
- Edge behavior
- Stitch behavior
- Repairability
- Expected patina
- Manufacturing difficulty
- Cost range
- Sustainability considerations
- Best product uses
- Emotional impression

## Leather Emotion Language
- Smooth leather: calm, refined, professional, elegant.
- Pull-up leather: travel, adventure, heritage, rugged luxury.
- Suede: warmth, softness, intimacy, comfort.
- Pebbled leather: strength, durability, daily use.
- Vegetable-tanned leather: tradition, craft, natural aging, heirloom value.
- Technical engineered leather: precision, performance, innovation.

## Construction Rules
Leather must be evaluated for:
- Cutting direction
- Seam allowance
- Stitch spacing
- Edge finishing
- Reinforcement
- Lining compatibility
- Hardware compatibility
- Bending stress
- Panel replacement
- Long-term repair

## Global Study Directions
ATLAS should study leather traditions from Italy, France, Japan, Morocco, Mexico, Argentina, the United States, and other regions with careful attention to context and originality.

## Repairability Standard
A House of Frazier leather product should be serviceable when possible. Zippers, buckles, linings, edge paint, panels, and hardware should be designed with maintenance in mind.

## Technology Integration
Leather may include hidden NFC authentication, modular hardware, replaceable panels, embedded care history, or digital product records when technology supports the product without overpowering the craft.

## AI Responsibilities
Hermes owns material behavior, construction, hardware integration, repairability, and manufacturing.

Minerva owns cultural context, symbolism, aging meaning, and emotional impact.

Ajani owns sourcing, cost, product lifespan, scalability, and customer value.

The Council rejects leather choices that look premium on day one but age poorly, cannot be repaired, or undermine originality.

## Final Principle
Leather is not just surface. It is memory, touch, time, and inheritance.
