# ATLAS Hardware Library

## Purpose
Teach ATLAS to treat hardware as the jewelry of engineering. Buttons, zippers, buckles, rivets, clasps, snaps, pull tabs, eyelets, hooks, locks, and fasteners should become recognizable elements of the House of Frazier design language.

## Core Philosophy
Hardware should never feel generic. It should function well, age well, feel good in the hand, and carry subtle story.

## Hardware Study Areas
- Buttons
- Zippers
- Snaps
- Buckles
- Rivets
- Clasps
- Pull tabs
- Hooks
- Eyelets
- Magnetic closures
- Bag feet
- Watch crowns
- Belt systems
- Modular connectors

## Evaluation Criteria
Every hardware component should be reviewed for:
- Function
- Weight
- Scale
- Finish
- Sound
- Temperature
- Grip
- Durability
- Repairability
- Replaceability
- Manufacturing method
- Material compatibility
- Safety
- Story potential
- Frazier identity

## Material Options
Hardware may use stainless steel, brass, bronze, titanium, aluminum, ceramics, carbon fiber, enamel, glass, stone, engineered polymers, or future materials when appropriate.

## Signature Hardware Direction
The House of Frazier should eventually develop original hardware families:
- Sapphire Knight fasteners
- Crimson Queen buttons
- Council clasps
- Archive rivets
- Forge buckles
- AIon zipper pulls

These should be subtle enough for elegance but distinct enough to become recognizable over time.

## Interaction Rule
Hardware is touched. ATLAS must consider tactile experience.

Questions:
- Does it feel satisfying to open?
- Does it make a cheap sound?
- Is the weight appropriate?
- Does it catch light beautifully?
- Can it be repaired or replaced?
- Does it support the garment or dominate it?

## Story Integration
Hardware can hide:
- AIon glyphs
- Collection numbers
- Micro-engraved phrases
- Chess silhouettes
- Symbolic edge geometry
- Hidden underside markings
- Authentication markers

## AI Responsibilities
Hermes owns function, material selection, durability, manufacturing, repair, and tactile quality.

Minerva owns symbolism, engraving language, story integration, and emotional meaning.

Ajani owns cost, supply chain, product line consistency, scalability, and long-term brand recognition.

The Council rejects hardware that is decorative but weak, beautiful but impractical, or too similar to another brand's signature identity.

## Final Principle
Hardware is where engineering becomes jewelry.
