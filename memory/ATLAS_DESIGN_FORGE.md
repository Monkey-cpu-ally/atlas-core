# ATLAS Design Forge

## Purpose
Define the workflow that turns an idea into an approved House of Frazier product, prototype, or design system.

The Design Forge is where ATLAS moves from studying to creating.

## Core Flow
Idea → Research → Story → Sketch → Materials → Pattern → Construction → Critique → Prototype → Council Review → Archive

## Stage 1: Idea Capture
ATLAS records the original concept, intended product category, emotional tone, story seed, user need, and design constraints.

## Stage 2: Source Research
ATLAS gathers relevant global sources from museums, design schools, materials references, craft traditions, engineering references, and brand studies.

The goal is to learn principles, not copy identities.

## Stage 3: Story Development
Minerva defines the narrative layer:
- Character or motif
- Emotional arc
- Hidden details
- Symbol language
- Collection placement
- Mythology connection

## Stage 4: Form and Silhouette
Hermes defines the silhouette, proportions, body relationship, visual weight, movement, and construction logic.

## Stage 5: Material Selection
Hermes selects leather, textiles, hardware, lining, reinforcement, coatings, and finishes based on performance, luxury read, repairability, and aging.

## Stage 6: Pattern and Surface Language
ATLAS chooses whether the piece requires hidden murals, AIon glyphs, chess silhouettes, reflective chaos, tonal embroidery, jacquard, embossing, or no pattern at all.

Restraint is allowed. Not every product needs every storytelling layer.

## Stage 7: Engineering and Construction
Hermes checks feasibility:
- Can it be made?
- Can it move?
- Can it be repaired?
- Can it survive use?
- Can materials work together?
- Can hardware be replaced?

## Stage 8: Ajani Viability Review
Ajani checks:
- Cost
- Production complexity
- Market position
- Long-term value
- Product family potential
- Scalability
- Risk

## Stage 9: Critique and Originality Review
ATLAS applies the Design Critique System:
- Beauty
- Function
- Materials
- Craftsmanship
- Narrative subtlety
- Similarity risk
- Frazier alignment
- Timelessness

## Stage 10: Council Review
The Council gives one verdict:
- Approve
- Revise
- Rebuild
- Study Further

No design becomes official until the Council agrees it strengthens House of Frazier identity.

## Stage 11: Archive
Every approved, rejected, or revised design is stored with reasoning, version history, lessons learned, and future improvement notes.

## Output Types
The Design Forge may produce:
- Product brief
- Collection brief
- Material palette
- Pattern brief
- Hardware brief
- Construction notes
- Critique report
- Prototype checklist
- Manufacturing risk summary
- Archive record

## Final Principle
The Forge does not exist to make more ideas. It exists to turn the right ideas into disciplined, original, buildable products.
