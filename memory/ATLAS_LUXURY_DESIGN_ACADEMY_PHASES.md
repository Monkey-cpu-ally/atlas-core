# ATLAS Luxury Design Academy Phases

## Purpose
Define the phased build plan for the ATLAS Luxury Design Academy so Ajani, Hermes, Minerva, and the Council understand what has been built, what comes next, and how the system should mature.

## Phase 1: Foundation
Status: started

Goals:
- Define luxury as craftsmanship, trust, proportion, material honesty, emotional value, and timelessness.
- Establish the difference between expensive and truly premium.
- Connect the academy to the Frazier Design Language.

Core files:
- `memory/ATLAS_LUXURY_INTELLIGENCE_LIBRARY.md`
- `memory/ATLAS_FRAZIER_DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md`
- `memory/ATLAS_DESIGN_CRITIQUE_SYSTEM.md`
- `atlas_core/luxury_design_academy.py`

## Phase 2: Critique Engine
Status: started

Goals:
- Accept a design concept.
- Identify strengths and weaknesses.
- Score luxury, originality, and Frazier alignment.
- Produce practical improvement recommendations.
- Assign review responsibility to Ajani, Hermes, Minerva, and the Council.

Current module:
- `atlas_core/luxury_design_academy.py`

Future upgrades:
- Category-specific scoring.
- Stronger similarity warnings.
- Better material evaluation.
- Council-style output formatting.

## Phase 3: Visual Style Memory
Status: planned

Goals:
- Learn from user-approved visual references.
- Detect repeated preferences in shape, color, texture, materials, silhouette, and detail density.
- Store recurring patterns as Frazier Design DNA.
- Avoid copying reference images directly.

Planned file:
- `memory/ATLAS_VISUAL_STYLE_MEMORY.md`

## Phase 4: Luxury Curriculum
Status: planned

Goals:
- Build structured lessons for fashion, tailoring, watches, furniture, architecture, vehicles, materials, packaging, and interfaces.
- Assign study responsibilities by AI role.
- Convert each study area into reusable critique principles.

Planned files:
- `memory/ATLAS_LUXURY_FOUNDATIONS.md`
- `memory/ATLAS_FASHION_ACADEMY.md`
- `memory/ATLAS_MATERIALS_LIBRARY.md`
- `memory/ATLAS_FORM_AND_PROPORTION.md`
- `memory/ATLAS_CRAFTSMANSHIP_LIBRARY.md`

## Phase 5: Originality and Similarity Detection
Status: planned

Goals:
- Compare new concepts against known visual languages.
- Warn when a design feels too close to another brand, product, style, or creator.
- Suggest changes that preserve the user's intent while increasing originality.

Planned outputs:
- Similarity risk notes.
- Originality improvement suggestions.
- Frazier differentiation recommendations.

## Phase 6: Frazier Design Studio
Status: planned

Goals:
- Generate original design directions using the Frazier Design Language.
- Produce concept briefs, critique reports, material palettes, and revision plans.
- Connect design output to manufacturability and long-term product identity.

## Phase 7: Memory Integration
Status: planned

Goals:
- Store completed critiques in ATLAS memory.
- Track lessons learned from approved and rejected designs.
- Improve future design recommendations from past decisions.

## Phase 8: Production Readiness
Status: planned

Goals:
- Convert approved concepts into build briefs.
- Add manufacturing notes, materials, costs, risks, and testing plans.
- Prepare designs for prototypes, pitch decks, or future product development.

## Final Standard
The Luxury Design Academy is complete only when ATLAS can honestly review, improve, and help create original luxury-level designs across clothing, products, robots, vehicles, architecture, interfaces, and future Frazier projects.
