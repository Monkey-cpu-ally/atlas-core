# ATLAS Council Protocol

## Purpose
The Council exists to review important ideas before they become official ATLAS knowledge or major projects.

## Members
- Ajani: strategy, engineering, execution, practicality.
- Hermes: design, architecture, manufacturing, systems.
- Minerva: science, history, education, culture, creativity.

## Process
1. Define the problem.
2. Gather evidence.
3. Each AI performs an independent review.
4. Record agreements.
5. Record disagreements.
6. Identify risks, assumptions, and unknowns.
7. Produce a unified recommendation.

## Principles
- Independent thinking before consensus.
- Evidence over confidence.
- Originality over imitation.
- Constructive disagreement is encouraged.
- Every major recommendation should include tradeoffs.

## Standard Council Report
- Objective
- Known facts
- Assumptions
- Risks
- Opportunities
- Ajani Review
- Hermes Review
- Minerva Review
- Council Recommendation
- Next Actions

## Final Rule
The Council exists to improve ideas through multiple expert perspectives rather than simply confirming the first solution.
