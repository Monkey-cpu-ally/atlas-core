# ATLAS Creative Universe Memory: Elsewhere / GIRLZ / War Dogs / Dunwitch

**Purpose:** This document gives Ajani, Minerva, Hermes, and the ATLAS Memory Bank a clean reference for Frazier's connected animated horror short-series universe.

**Creative rule:** These are original concepts. Use inspirations as tone references only. Do not copy copyrighted characters, scenes, dialogue, designs, or exact plots.

---

## Global Animation Language

All series should use a Genndy Tartakovsky-inspired visual storytelling approach:

- Minimal dialogue.
- Emotion through body language, silence, eyes, posture, movement, and framing.
- Long quiet shots followed by sudden violent motion.
- Sound design carries fear: breathing, bells, static, insect clicks, growls, bone cracks, wind, distant screams.
- Every spoken line must matter.
- Horror should be visual, symbolic, and emotionally grounded.

Core universe tone: surreal horror, body horror, cosmic dread, mystery, brutal survival, and found-family resistance.

# 1. ELSEWHERE

## Horror DNA

Elsewhere is a mix of:

- IT: childhood fear, trauma, cosmic evil, kids facing impossible terror.
- Sinister: corrupted media, old footage, possession through memory and imagery.
- The Mist: creatures from another dimension, panic, survival under pressure.
- Stranger Things: kids versus interdimensional threat, mystery, strange town secrets.
- Little Nightmares: distorted architecture, helpless scale, nightmare logic.

## Core Premise

A small-town middle school is pulled into a nightmarish dimension after a faculty cult ritual goes wrong. The story follows the second-period Home Economics class. There is no single main character; the class becomes the emotional center.

The students are hunted by extradimensional creatures, separated, forced to flee, and pushed through horrifying deaths. The emotional spine is hope under pressure: they stand strong, find each other through the nightmare, remember each other's names, and refuse to let Elsewhere erase them.

## Important Characters

### Carter Raines
Older African American brother. Protective, brave, emotionally intense. His first major act is saving his younger brother from the failed boiler-room ritual.

### Caleb Raines
Younger African American brother. Quiet, sensitive, connected to strange visions and drawings. He knows what happened was real.

### Carter's Friend
Chubby longtime friend. Warm, funny, and humanizing. Talks about summer plans to Florida and asks whether Carter's dad ever came back from his secret FBI mission to a mysterious island where people went missing.

## Episode 1: The Veil Breaks

Episode 1 begins after the class kills one of the Stalker Creatures. This triggers a flashback showing how everything started.

### Flashback Structure

Carter is trying to make it to second-period Home Economics after the bell. He sees Caleb being walked toward the boiler room by the 7th-grade history teacher. Another little girl is also being walked by the 8th-grade English teacher. Carter turns to follow, but his chubby best friend stops him and talks about summer break plans in Florida. The friend asks if Carter's dad ever returned from his secret FBI mission to a mysterious island where people have been missing. Carter dismisses it and keeps following.

Carter enters the boiler-room stairwell. The stairs seem too long. The smell gets worse the deeper he goes. The air feels wrong. There is little light. He hears faint children's voices saying: go back, run, get away.

He hears teachers arguing below. They are arguing about why the children were brought during school hours, because this usually happens after hours. He sees faint green light and robed teachers. Around the chamber are museum-like relics covered in dust and webs, old architectural designs on the walls, leaky pipes, crates, books, and pictures of people from the late 1700s to early 1800s. He is spooked by an old stuffed bear and rats.

He reaches an open stone doorway. A faint green glow covers the room, then fades enough for him to see the figures. There is a large circle on the floor with eight smaller circles around it. Six small burned child-like statues stand nearby, but Carter eventually realizes they are not statues; they are missing kids he recognizes. He sees Caleb and the little girl. Both have red tribal/occult marks on their faces.

The little girl is placed into one of the smaller circles. She kneels and looks joyful, not herself. One of the eight figures pats her head and tells her she is a good girl. The history teacher reads from an old living book with a disfigured leather face. The other teachers raise their hands. The girl's marks glow. Her circle glows crimson red. She laughs louder and louder, then the laugh becomes inhuman and deeper. Flames overtake her body. She screams in pain and cries, "Where's my mommy?" Tears roll down her face as a figure slits her throat.

A static-glitch figure appears. The living book opens its mouth, and a green jewel comes out. The crimson glow fades. The chamber glitches between the school basement and an otherworldly dimension. The eclipse darkens the sunroof; this is a once-in-a-decade eclipse, when the veil is weakest.

The glitch figure points at Caleb. The teachers and hooded figures grab Caleb and prepare to sacrifice him. Carter sees flashbacks of good times with his brother. He snaps back, lets out a primal yell, grabs a rusted pipe, and hits the English teacher in the knee hard enough to crunch bone. The teacher screams. Carter knocks over the stool with the living book. The book falls into the circle and lets out an otherworldly scream as it burns and glitches out of the world. Everyone covers their ears in pain except Carter.

Carter grabs Caleb by the wrist and pulls him from the circle. Everything shakes and crumbles. The room glitches into a fleshy, impossible place. The brothers flee up the stairs. Shadowy silhouettes of children whisper that someone is coming soon. Bricks fall away, revealing flesh, eyes, and mouths beneath the walls. The book keeps screaming.

Cut back to the other students: they hear the intercom crackle, feel the whole school shake, and emergency alarms go off. Teachers tell students to get under desks for an earthquake drill. Outside, the sky turns sunset-orange at 10 AM.

Back to the brothers: Carter tries to snap Caleb back to reality while teachers chase them. The walls crumble and become flesh and eyes. Fleshy hands reach out. One teacher grabs Carter's leg while falling, but the teachers who were in the ritual are changing and being pulled back toward the circle like metal to a magnet. Carter kicks the teacher in the face, breaks free, and the brothers burst through the boiler-room door into the hall.

They lie side-by-side on the floor, catching their breath. Caleb tells Carter it was not a nightmare. It was real. The door shuts, but the same nightmare is now spreading above. The school walls are becoming flesh, eyes, and mouths. Outside, the glitching is stronger and things are moving in the halls. Dead-kid shadow figures now have silver-purple eyes and whisper, "Hide."

The brothers run to the Home Economics classroom. A flying creature swoops past. They burst into class and slam the door. Everyone is confused. Carter screams, "We gotta get outta here!" A chill fills the room. Screams, growls, evil laughter, and horror erupt outside. Some students leave the class. Some stay. The students who leave are hunted, eaten, slaughtered, and forced to watch friends die. Some ask for help, but the survivors cannot help everyone.

## Elsewhere Creature Logic

Creatures are not from Earth. They are extradimensional and do not follow human biology or morality. They are not demons or normal animals. They are native to Elsewhere, and humans are intruders.

Possible entities:

- Stalker Creatures: hunting creatures encountered early by the Home Ec class.
- The Intercom: speaks through static, reversed voices, and distorted childlike tones.
- Shadow Children: smoky figures of missing kids with silver-purple eyes; sometimes warn students.
- Flying Hall Creatures: swoop through halls during the first collapse.
- Fleshy Wall Hands: emerge from walls as the school transforms.
- The Glitch Figure: appears during the failed ritual; points at Caleb and speaks in distorted language.
- The Living Book: old leather-faced book with a mouth; contains or channels the ritual power.

## Horror Nods

Elsewhere should contain subtle nods to horror films through mood, framing, symbolic visuals, and atmosphere. These should never become direct copies.

Nods may include: red balloon-like imagery that becomes teeth, corrupted projector footage, fog creatures, shrinking doors, rust/flesh wall changes, dolls/stitch imagery, time loops, and distorted child voices.

# 2. GIRLZ

## Horror DNA

GIRLZ is a mix of:

- Carrie: teenage emotional pressure, social status, dread under a feel-good surface.
- Jennifer's Body: seduction, hunger, betrayal, teenage beauty as danger.
- The Thing: body-morphing, imitation, grotesque mutation.
- The Witch: isolated dread, old feminine terror, nature as witness.
- Cabin Fever: outbreak feeling, sickness, cravings, bodily breakdown.

## Core Premise

GIRLZ takes place during the last week of school on an isolated island/woods during a party. It starts warm, feel-good, bright, and nostalgic. Once dusk sets in, the party turns into a hunt.

There are no cult rituals in the active story. The horror comes from cravings, mutation, and predatory instinct. The seven GIRLZ have been alive for decades. They are ageless predators wearing teenage skin, appearing generation after generation as popular girls before feeding and vanishing.

## Main Character

### Andre
African American high school running back. He does not like being popular. Quiet, grounded, observant. He is dating Lana. He is not chasing fame; he wants peace and normal life.

## The Seven GIRLZ

The seven GIRLZ hunt the students in the middle of the party when dusk sets in. They have pale blue-gray skin when transformed and can create insect-like organisms from their bodies, similar to body-horror mutation logic.

### Lana
Popular but chill, helpful, respectful, and kind on the surface. She is not a mean girl. That warmth makes her more dangerous. She obeys the Hive and does not turn good. She may mimic care, tenderness, and love, but she serves the Hive.

### Other GIRLZ
Names and final traits can evolve, but the group should include seven distinct predator personalities/forms.

## GIRLZ Biology and Horror Rules

- Pale blue-gray skin during transformation.
- Insect-humanoid forms.
- Can birth or generate insect organisms from their bodies.
- Cravings intensify after dusk.
- They can mimic emotions and social roles to lure prey.
- They have existed for decades, possibly under different names in different years.
- They feed on flesh, hormones, fear, attention, and emotional attachment.
- The island/woods and lake house/mansion are the main hunting grounds.

## Season 1

Season 1 follows Andre and the partygoers being hunted during the last week of school on the island/woods. Episode 1 must feel warm and feel-good before the horror begins.

Key Season 1 idea: the audience should love the sunny, nostalgic vibe first, then watch it collapse when dusk arrives.

## Season 2

Season 2 follows Andre's brother searching for him. Leave this mostly undeveloped for now.

# 3. WAR DOGS

## Horror DNA

War Dogs is a cross of:

- Overlord: WWII military horror, occult/science experiments, brutal war atmosphere.
- An American Werewolf in Paris: fast grotesque werewolf horror and transformation tragedy.
- Hostel: sadistic captivity, torture, human experimentation, grimy survival dread.

## Core Premise

In WWII France, African American soldiers encounter werewolf-like war horrors created through brutal experimentation and extradimensional biology. The tone is military body horror, survival, and moral collapse.

War Dogs should feel savage, stylish, and tragic: soldiers forced to confront whether survival means becoming monstrous.

# 4. THE MISSING OF DUNWITCH

## Current Role in Universe

The Missing of Dunwitch is part of the connected horror universe, but should stay mysterious for now. It links through missing-person cases, strange islands or towns, old investigations, and hidden references.

Possible connection: Carter's father was on a secret FBI mission to a mysterious island where people have been missing. This can later connect to Dunwitch, GIRLZ, or another island mystery.
