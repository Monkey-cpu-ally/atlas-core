# ATLAS Textile Academy

## Purpose
Teach ATLAS how fabrics behave, move, age, sound, reflect light, carry story, and affect the luxury perception of a garment or product.

Hermes should not only ask what fabric is used. Hermes should ask why that fabric belongs in the design.

## Core Study Areas
- Fiber type
- Weave
- Knit structure
- Weight
- Drape
- Stretch
- Breathability
- Warmth
- Moisture behavior
- Wrinkle behavior
- Sound
- Shine
- Texture
- Aging
- Repairability
- Sustainability
- Compatibility with leather, hardware, lining, and technology

## Fabric Emotion Language
- Linen: honest, relaxed, breathable, natural, timeless.
- Wool: intelligent, warm, structured, sophisticated.
- Cashmere: softness, comfort, quiet luxury, rarity.
- Silk: elegance, movement, lightness, ceremony.
- Denim: authenticity, durability, youth, heritage.
- Canvas: exploration, work, strength, utility.
- Technical textiles: precision, performance, protection, innovation.

## Fabric Under Light
ATLAS should evaluate fabric under sunlight, shadow, indoor lighting, stage lighting, rain, moonlight, and reflective conditions. The same fabric can feel different depending on light.

## Movement Rules
Fabric should be studied by how it moves:
- Does it flow?
- Does it stand away from the body?
- Does it fold beautifully?
- Does it hold structure?
- Does it wrinkle with character or failure?
- Does it support the intended silhouette?

## Sound Rule
The sound of clothing matters.

ATLAS should record whether a textile is silent, whispering, crisp, heavy, sharp, soft, or noisy. Sound contributes to perceived quality and comfort.

## Layering Rules
Fabrics should be judged in combination, not isolation.

ATLAS should evaluate:
- Outer fabric
- Lining
- Interlining
- Reinforcement
- Hidden mural layers
- Reflective layers
- Comfort layers
- Weather layers

## Hidden Storytelling
Textiles are ideal for hidden House of Frazier storytelling through:
- Jacquard patterns
- Tonal embroidery
- Hidden linings
- Thread-on-thread symbols
- Quilted relief
- Reflective motifs
- AIon glyphs
- Micro woven story fragments

## AI Responsibilities
Hermes owns fabric behavior, construction compatibility, performance, comfort, and manufacturability.

Minerva owns symbolic meaning, history, cultural context, emotional tone, and story integration.

Ajani owns sourcing, cost, durability, production planning, and customer value.

The Council rejects fabric choices that look good but fail comfort, aging, construction, or story integration.

## Final Principle
Fabric is not flat. It is movement, light, sound, temperature, and story.
