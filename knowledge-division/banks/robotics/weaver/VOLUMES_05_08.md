# Weaver Engineering Bible — Volumes 5–8

## Volume 5 — Vision-Based Quality Inspection
Inspection systems verify dimensions, surfaces, assembly correctness, connector seating, cable routing, fasteners, labels, and critical tolerances.

Defects are classified as:
- Cosmetic
- Functional
- Critical
- Repairable
- Scrap
- Human-review required

Every inspected part receives images, measurements, pass/fail status, timestamp, production history, and Digital Twin linkage.

## Volume 6 — Autonomous Factory Operations
Hermes coordinates production, materials, inventory, scheduling, maintenance, quality, energy use, and human collaboration.

Factory principles:
1. Flow before speed
2. Quality before quantity
3. Reliability before maximum output
4. Safety before deadlines
5. Continuous improvement

Production schedules remain dynamic and adapt to material delays, maintenance, quality issues, priority changes, and environmental conditions.

## Volume 7 — Predictive Maintenance and Self-Diagnostics
Critical systems report vibration, temperature, current draw, backlash, motion consistency, communication reliability, tool condition, and environmental context.

Maintenance planning considers condition, production priority, technicians, spare parts, and downtime impact.

Startup and shutdown include controlled diagnostic checks and preserved reports.

## Volume 8 — Digital Twin Manufacturing Network
Every asset receives a digital identity containing configuration, revision history, components, software, calibration, production, inspection, and maintenance records.

Hierarchy:
- ATLAS manufacturing campus
- Factory
- Production cell
- Machine
- Subsystem
- Component

Digital Twins support monitoring, simulation, configuration management, lifecycle engineering, troubleshooting, training, and Knowledge Bank integration.
