# Weaver Engineering Bible — Volumes 9–11

## Volume 9 — Autonomous Material Logistics and Intelligent Inventory
Hermes tracks receiving, inspection, approved storage, internal transport, work-in-progress, finished goods, spare parts, recycling, and reorder planning.

Every material has:
- Identity
- Supplier and batch data
- Location
- Quantity
- Condition
- Inspection status
- Approved uses
- Digital Twin link

## Volume 10 — Precision Assembly Intelligence
Assemblies are organized as product, major assembly, subassembly, component, and individual part.

Hermes plans sequence, tools, materials, arm assignments, inspection checkpoints, alignment requirements, error recovery, functional testing, and traceability.

In-process verification covers fit, orientation, fasteners, connectors, cable routing, component identity, and critical dimensions.

## Volume 11 — WeaverOS
WeaverOS coordinates:
- Manufacturing missions
- Production scheduling
- Resource allocation
- Machine management
- Quality
- Maintenance
- Safety
- Digital Twin synchronization
- Knowledge Bank updates

Operating layers:
1. Engineering goals
2. Production planning
3. Manufacturing coordination
4. Machine management
5. Motion control
6. Embedded controllers
7. Sensors and actuators

Every decision should be explainable, measurable, traceable, recoverable, safe, modular, and scalable.
