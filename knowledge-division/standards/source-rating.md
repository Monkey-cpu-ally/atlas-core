# Source Rating Standard

ATLAS must rate sources before storing claims as knowledge.

## A-Level Source
Strongest category.

Examples:
- Official standards
- Official documentation
- Peer-reviewed papers
- Government or university datasets
- Manufacturer technical manuals

Use for facts that need high trust.

## B-Level Source
Strong but not perfect.

Examples:
- University explainers
- Textbooks
- Expert books
- Reputable technical publications
- Professional lectures

Use for learning and technical background.

## C-Level Source
Useful but needs verification.

Examples:
- Reliable journalism
- High-quality documentaries
- Expert interviews
- Conference talks
- Well-sourced videos

Use for context, not final proof.

## D-Level Source
Weak source.

Examples:
- Blogs
- Forums
- Social media
- Marketing pages
- Unsourced videos

Use only as a lead for deeper research.

## F-Level Source
Do not trust without major verification.

Examples:
- Rumors
- Fake quotes
- Unsupported claims
- Hype posts
- AI-generated pages with no sources
- Conspiracy-style claims

## Rule
A claim should not become `Verified` unless it has strong support from A-Level or B-Level sources.
