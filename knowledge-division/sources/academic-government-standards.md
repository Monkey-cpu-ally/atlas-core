# Academic, Government, and Standards Sources

These are the strongest sources for verified knowledge.

## Academic Sources

Academic sources are used for fundamentals, research, theory, experiments, and expert teaching.

Examples:

```text
Universities
Research institutes
Open courseware
Peer-reviewed papers
Conference proceedings
Dissertations
Laboratory publications
Textbooks
```

Primary Use:

- Science
- Engineering
- Medicine
- Mathematics
- AI
- Robotics
- Environmental research
- History and philosophy when scholarly

Trust Tier:
Tier A or Tier B depending on source.

## Government Sources

Government sources provide public data, safety guidance, large-scale measurements, and official research.

Examples:

```text
NASA
NOAA
CDC
NIH
USGS
DOE
NIST
EPA
Census data
Agricultural agencies
Transportation agencies
National laboratories
```

Primary Use:

- Space
- Weather
- Public health
- Energy
- Environmental science
- Geology
- Standards
- Safety
- Public datasets

Trust Tier:
Tier A for official data and documentation, Tier B/C for interpretive reports depending on context.

## Standards Sources

Standards sources define how professionals build, test, measure, certify, and protect systems.

Examples:

```text
ISO
IEC
IEEE
ASTM
ASME
ANSI
NIST
UL
NFPA
SAE
Building codes
Medical device standards
Software security standards
```

Primary Use:

- Engineering design
- Electrical safety
- Manufacturing quality
- Materials testing
- Building design
- Software systems
- Medical device safety
- Risk review

Trust Tier:
Tier A.

## ATLAS Rule

When a creator, article, video, or blog makes a major claim, ATLAS should attempt to verify it using academic, government, standards, or official documentation sources.

## Source Review Questions

```text
Who published it?
What institution backs it?
Is it peer-reviewed or official?
Is it current?
Does it cite data?
Does it include limitations?
Can stronger sources confirm it?
Is it relevant to an ATLAS project?
```
