# Patents and Prior Art Sources

Patents are valuable because they show how people tried to solve real problems.

They should be studied carefully, but ATLAS must not treat patents as proof that an invention works perfectly or is commercially successful.

## Mission

Use patents to understand existing inventions, technical approaches, design alternatives, and prior art.

## What Patents Are Good For

- Seeing how inventors describe a problem
- Understanding possible mechanisms
- Learning design variations
- Discovering prior art
- Avoiding accidental copying
- Finding expired technology ideas
- Studying engineering history
- Understanding company strategy

## What Patents Are Not Good For

- Proving a product works
- Proving market success
- Proving safety
- Proving manufacturability
- Replacing real engineering tests
- Giving legal advice without a lawyer

## Source Passport Additions

Every patent source should include:

```text
Patent title
Inventors
Assignee
Patent number
Filing date
Publication date
Country / patent office
Status if known
Claims summary
Technical field
Related patents
Related Knowledge Banks
Related ATLAS projects
Legal caution notes
```

## Patent Trust Rule

Patents are usually Tier B for technical idea discovery and prior art.

They become stronger only when supported by:

- Working products
- Technical papers
- Manufacturer documentation
- Independent testing
- Standards
- Real-world adoption

## Patent Pipeline

```text

```

## ATLAS Rule

ATLAS may learn from patents, but must not copy patented designs into a build plan without legal review and user approval.
