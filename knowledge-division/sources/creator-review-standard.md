# Creator Review Standard

Independent creators can be useful, but they must be reviewed carefully.

## Mission

Let ATLAS learn from skilled educators, builders, artists, analysts, and creators without confusing explanation, opinion, entertainment, or speculation with verified evidence.

## Creator Categories

```text
Technical educator
Engineering demonstrator
Programming educator
Science communicator
Business analyst
History explainer
Design critic
Art educator
Architecture educator
Manufacturing demonstrator
Idea discovery creator
Community signal source
```

## Review Criteria

Each creator should be reviewed for:

- Expertise
- Accuracy
- Transparency
- Evidence use
- Correction history
- Bias
- Hype level
- Practical usefulness
- Consistency
- Whether claims can be verified elsewhere

## Creator Trust Ratings

```text
A-Level: Expert with strong evidence, citations, credentials, and reliable corrections
B-Level: Strong educator with mostly reliable explanations
C-Level: Useful idea/trend source but claims need verification
D-Level: Entertainment or weak evidence source
F-Level: Misleading, unsafe, conspiratorial, plagiarized, or unreliable
```

## Creator Passport

Every approved creator should receive:

```text
Creator name
Channel / site
Primary topics
Knowledge Banks
Trust role
Evidence rating
Useful for
Not useful for
Known weaknesses
Verification rule
Council confidence
Last reviewed
```

## ATLAS Rule

Creators can introduce topics. Stronger sources must confirm important claims.

## ColdFusion Example

```text
Creator: ColdFusion
Trust Role: Idea Discovery
Useful For: Technology history, innovation trends, business failures, corruption case studies
Not Useful For: Final engineering proof, investment decisions, medical claims, legal conclusions
Rule: Verify major claims with primary or professional sources
```
